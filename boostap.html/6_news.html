Mahatma Gandhi - The Power of Non-Violence

Mahatma Gandhi believed that freedom could be won without hatred or violence. He led movements like the Dandi March, where he walked miles to protest British salt laws. Even when he was beaten or jailed, he never fought back. His truth and non-violence united millions and showed the world a new way to fight injustice.

Bhagat Singh was a brave young freedom fighter who believed in bold action. To protest unfair British laws, he threw non-lethal bombs in the assembly and surrendered willingly. He wanted his voice to be heard, not to hurt anyone. At just 23 years old, he was hanged, smiling and chanting slogans for freedom.
