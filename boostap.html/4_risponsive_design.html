The Thirsty Crow
One hot summer day, a thirsty crow found a pitcher with very little water at the bottom. Unable to reach the water, the crow intelligently dropped pebbles into the pitcher one by one, causing the water level to rise until he could drink it. Moral: Intelligence and persistence pay off.
The Tortoise and the Hare
Confident in his speed, a hare challenges a slow-moving tortoise to a race. The hare takes a nap, believing he has plenty of time, while the tortoise keeps walking slowly and steadily. The tortoise wins the race while the hare is sleeping. Moral: Slow and steady wins the race.
The Fox and the Grapes
The Fox and the Grapes A fox sees a bunch of juicy grapes hanging high on a vine. He jumps repeatedly to catch them but fails. Tired and frustrated, he walks away, saying, "They were probably sour anyway." Moral: It is easy to despise what you cannot get.
The Ant and the Grasshopper
During the summer, an ant works hard to store food for the winter, while a grasshopper spends his time singing and dancing. When winter arrives, the ant is warm and fed, while the grasshopper is cold and starving. Moral: Work hard today to prepare for tomorrow.
The Boy Who Cried Wolf
A bored shepherd boy lies to the villagers by crying "Wolf!" twice to watch them run, only to find no wolf. When a real wolf actually appears, the villagers do not believe his cries, and the wolf eats the sheep. Moral: Nobody believes a liar, even when they are telling the truth.
he Goose That Laid the Golden Eggs
A farmer owns a magical goose that lays one solid golden egg every day, making him rich. Driven by greed and wanting all the gold at once, the farmer kills the goose to get the eggs inside, only to find it is just like any other goose, losing his source of wealth forever. Moral: Greed leads to loss.
